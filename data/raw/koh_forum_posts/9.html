That video isn't doing well in views because it's LONG - it's because it's a negatively titled review about Sea of Thieves, a game that has imploded over the last week, and everyone on the internet is looking for negative reviews of it on YouTube. It "fell into the YouTube search algorithm" which ONLY promotes negative videos, and is getting views based off of that. Ad-wise, it's also doing well, but only because of that fact.

          My gameplay videos don't fall into the YouTube search algorithm, not because they're short, but because they're just raw gameplay and that doesn't "fit the criteria" to get pushed. So their length won't affect that, at all.

          My overall views are not down, despite the fact that people are finally micro-analyzing the views on every video I upload (Which I haven't done for years). So of course stuff would be down then, but they've already picked up bigtime in March (in about 3 weeks I've already gotten as many views as I did the entire month of February).

Does it benefit the viewer? A little bit, because there's less parts. Does it benefit me? NO, IT CONCRETELY HURTS ME. I tried it for 2 years and firsthand saw it was a failed experiment. It works for other styles of videos, but NOT for raw gameplay, period.

          These are all observations based on facts. Show me otherwise and I'll gladly consider it.
