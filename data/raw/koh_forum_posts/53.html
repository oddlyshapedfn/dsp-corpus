Sadly, that's INCREDIBLY unrealistic. Even working an office job for 5 years, I wasn't making as much as I am now on YouTube (even AFTER the pay cut I'm going to take) and the fact is, the bills aren't going to go away.

           

          Taking focus AWAY from YouTube right now to grab a job in some desperate attempt at a more "certain" income would be ultimately futile, because the dip in YouTube performance/Patron support I'd see would FAR outweigh any consistent income I'd get from the job. I don't care what kind of positive you view you have of the United States job scene right now; since no matter what I'd be doing would be entry-level after being OUT of the job market for 5+ years, it simply would not make me enough money to warrant putting so much less time/effort into YouTube and I'd basically go bankrupt, being unable to pay my bills and getting everything foreclosed on, while ruining my credit in the process.

          DSP mentioned on Achievement Hunter

          in General

          Posted November 25, 2015

          18 hours ago, Wanglord said:

          Plenty of people on Youtube do animated series by themselves. Egoraptor built his fanbase on making animated videos. 

          Main difference between DSP and most other very popular Youtubers. DSP is all about quantity, most others are about quality. DSP "complains" (read: mentions it on Twitter) when he edits for a couple of hours; many Youtubers put 10+ hours into one video.

          Not really, actually.

          I'm all about gaming. I'd much rather sit in front of my TV gaming and sharing those experiences with others, than editing. Not that editing doesn't have its appeal; putting in a lot of time to pump out a high-quality video (read: looks nice because of production values) feels great if people then watch and appreciate it. And it feels cool to watch back a video I put time into editing and see people react to it.

          But I'm not a video editor, don't really want to be a full-time one either. I'm a gamer at heart and that's what I focus on: sharing those core gameplay experiences with others. I didn't go to school for editing, and have zero training; everything I've ever edited was done on-the-fly with self-taught skills, just messing around in vid editor programs myself (or recently utilizing a few online tutorials). So when people criticize something I've edited, I HAVE to laugh because it's almost as if they think I know what I'm doing; not that I haven't gotten better over the years, but again, not my major focus.

          It just so happens that PLAYING GAMES A LOT and sharing those experiences with others daily has turned into a successful business (that would be doing fine right now it if weren't for malicious lawbreaking trolls). But ultimately it's true that the average YouTube viewer IS now looking for something with more production quality, that looks more like a highly-edited TV show than just gameplay. So that's why I might be heading in a different direction in 2016, or at least, having more focus towards that.


          IF I'd had money, or training, or even taken any of the videos I made seriously pre-layoff in late 2010, things probably would have turned out way differently. But people seem to forget that this was a HOBBY I did for over 2 years before I ever made a cent doing it, and sadly I didn't make enough to hire a production team, nor found anyone who wanted a long-term business relationship. People like John and Howard loved being in my videos for a time, but they never made it a major focus of their lives; they always had their own stuff going on and wanted to focus on that. John in particular enjoyed doing co-op but always had other aspirations; he actually STOPPED doing gameplay on his own YouTube channel after a time despite it being pretty incredibly popular with people. So I never actually found a "team" of people who wanted to do this kind of thing long-term.

           

          Ultimately, if you want my honest opinion, I'd value someone whose translated 7+ years of daily gameplay into a successful business (despite not having real editing skills) far more than someone who just sat there editing for 10+ hours into every video, and only released a few a week. That's my personal preference; I see through fancy effects, transitions, and presentation and see the core value of a video for what it is. I really couldn't give a shit about a "rant vlog" from someone who doesn't have something to add to a topic besides drama (which tons of people do), and I certainly don't think an "edited" video of staged gameplay - with jokes added to sound like a TV comedy - is better than someone legitimately playing a game WITHOUT all that fake stuff.

          Not to say that's what RoosterTeeth does, because I have no idea. I'm just saying, that's not me as I wasn't lucky enough to be in a situation where 1. I had video editing experience and 2. I found a group of people who wanted to work with me, to make a YouTube gaming business experience work. They did, and more power to them. They certainly handled the Achievement Hunter thing the proper way to not be disrespectful to me (which is the actual topic here, I thought???)
