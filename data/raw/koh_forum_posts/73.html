          It's quite easy for me to attempt to make a change, because I'm one person. It's impossible to ask an amalgamous, uneducated, selfish bandwagon movement to do so, despite the fact we all now see how detrimental it was to my business overall.

           

          To whtevrwt: You're utterly wrong. These montages are COMPLETELY out of context because they're a representation of 0.000001% of the content I've put out there in 6 years of my life. 6 YEARS of videos, where 20 minutes of things I've said that are negative are compiled into one video. I'm not saying I didn't say them, nor that they weren't wrong to say - I'm saying every human on planet Earth does it, but I had the balls to share it all, the visceral REAL me instead of the edited/PC-version that a lot of popular YouTubers portray in their vids. That's why I call myself "the realest guy on the internet." I don't hide my faults.

          The problem is someone from the outside, who never watched my content and has no context of who I am, who just watches a 20 minute montage of my worst moments/faults. That is a completely improper, one-sided, out of context representation of who I am and it's become THE NORM of what people think of me. That's the definition of "slanderous/one-sided/out-of-context" if you didn't know.

           

          One final point here: don't ever be fooled into thinking anyone who did one of these negative montages of me tried to do it "to expose Phil so he'll see his faults and improve." That's utter bullshit. Because if someone ever had such a HUGE problem with my or my behavior, they would either 1. stop watching me or 2. talk to me about it personally. I have a Twitter, I have a Facebook, I have a personal email address that's public, etc. that are all direct lines to me. If they actually cared, they'd know I have a business that is my livelihood and doing something that's intentionally slanderous HURTS ME; last time I checked, you don't intentionally hurt people who like or care about.

          Doing a public, one-sided and slanderous representation of me in a video is done SOLELY for the purpose of causing drama and getting views for personal gain.           On the case of Phil's Plummeting Popularity and Morals

          in Suggestions

          Posted January 4, 2015

          I don't get why people don't understand my reasoning when it comes to donations, e-begging, and the like because I've probably talked about it around 100 times now. But I guess people either don't listen or only hear what they want to hear to form their own notions of who I am, or that I have a huge hypocritical persona, or something.

          I don't think anyone who is making a legit, stable living on YouTube ad revenue (or a contract w/Machinima or another company like me) should be accepting monetary donations, period. If you're doing well off for yourself, why would you ask for/accept more? Name another occupation where this is accepted - do you tip your well-off doctor? Does Beyonce have a "tip jar" where you can send her money? Of course not. These people are known to be making a great living and it would be immoral for them to do so. The difference here is that YouTubers aren't PUBLICLY KNOWN (with the exception of top guys like Pewdiepie) to be well-off and usually don't disclose their income.

           

          I'll give you a hint: if anyone on YouTube has been getting views around what I got until I moved this past year (June 2014 is when I saw the big decline overall), they have been making a great to decent living off of ad revenue/contracts. If they've accepted money or significant donations from fans, that was just bonus money they really didn't need, but took nonetheless. I accepted donations BEFORE I lost my job via Paypal, and then accepted them only until February of 2011 when my contact with Machinima went into effect. When I saw what kind of money I was going to make consistently, I immediately felt that it would be morally wrong to accept donations and CLOSED the paypal donation link. I also refunded multiple donations that I received and haven't accepted one since.

          Did I accept Twitch subs? Yes, but that was a tradeoff (and not an even one) for the YouTube views that I lost when I started live streaming. In the long-run it did not offset the drops in views I got on DSPGaming and ultimately it was a loss that I took for the sake of fanservice for the viewers.

          In actuality, most YouTubers of my popularity or more are making a damn good living. You just don't know it, and we can't talk about it because our contracts have a non-disclosure agreement.

           

          Now, in regards to Twitch and e-begging: it's pretty well known that multiple YouTubers/major streamers ONLY stream on Twitch for the sake of money. They're not doing legit playthroughs but instead "Fan interaction" streams where they constantly ask/beg for more subs, or donations via Patreon/Paypal. They run commercial breaks constantly and when someone does donate they interrupt the entire stream to "play a sound" and sometimes even constantly give a shoutout. THIS IS COMMONPLACE on Twitch and I don't have to have watched a million other streamers to know it. I was one of the people who felt that my content, while streaming, should be of the SAME caliber as when I was doing it for YouTube and should not be constantly interrupted for the sake of making money; I wanted to create an artistic work that entertained you and in turn, I'd make some money doing it. I did NOT stream to make money and it's my personal philosophy that those that do those worthless streams are only in it for the money, and not to actually make great content.

           

          I do hold myself in personal regard as someone who started doing this in 2008 for the love of the game, and did it for 2+ years before I made a penny on it. Things may have changed in regards to the format/quality of my work, but it's still done for the same of the enjoyment of the game - NOT to make a dollar. Making money is always the second thought to having an honest/legit playthrough of a game. There will always be exceptions (Getting horribly stuck and using stream chat for example) but overall I'd like to think that when you watch me play a game on stream OR on YouTube, you're seeing a pure, unedited, honest experience. Not an overproduced/edited montage to make me look good/funny, or a "fan service" stream that's just a cover to beg for money.

           

          You may completely disagree with my feelings on donations and the like, and that's okay; but don't hold me in a negative light because I hold myself up to a personal standard that other people that you watch and like may not. 

          I am going to begin using Patreon this year for a simple reason: due to a slanderous movement against me (whether based on fact in some cases or on complete misinformation/lies in others doesn't matter) my viewership has dropped and it's very difficult for me to even get new viewers anymore. When all you find on YouTube when you search for DSP, DSP Gaming or DarksydePhil is "this is how you don't play" or "this is why I hate Phil" videos, it pretty much destroys any hope of recovering from a natural system. I'm in a unique part of my life where I just moved, have a bunch of debt, and need to pay it down; right now I need help with this, and my fans/viewers have been asking for YEARS for me to start accepting donations. At the very least, I'm going to do Patreon rather than just accepting blanket Paypal donations so that they get something back for their contributions.

          If my viewership were to increase back to where I was, I would again refuse donations. But after 6 years of doing this, this is the reality I'm facing. Saying foolish things like "just get a job and do this as a side thing again" is incredibly uneducated and immature; nobody is going to hire me for any job similar to what I used to do, where I could bring in enough to balance work/YouTube and make the same amount of money that I do now. So this is just a logical step in the progression of my business and my content. It's not hypocritical, it's mandatory.
