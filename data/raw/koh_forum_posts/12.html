          in General

          Posted December 29, 2017

          What is the minimum wage for workers who receive tips?

          The Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) requires payment of at least the Federal minimum wage to covered, nonexempt employees.  An employer of a tipped employee is only required to pay $2.13 an hour in direct wages if that amount plus the tips received equals at least the Federal minimum wage, the employee retains all tips and the employee customarily and regularly receives more than $30 a month in tips. If an employee's tips combined with the employer's direct wages of at least $2.13 an hour do not equal the Federal minimum hourly wage, the employer must make up the difference.

          So, if you worked for a restaurant and only made $2.13 an hour, and you didn't make enough tips to equate to whatever the federal minimum wage is, the employer is FEDERALLY REQUIRED to pay the difference. If they didn't, guess what? Your employer broke the law, and a simple reporting would probably get them massively fined and back in line with what they're supposed to be doing. The problem is, people don't inform themselves on their rights and then allow themselves to get mistreated on the regular. In reality, a busy restaurant with good service allows waiters/waitresses to earn far more than the federal minimum wage through large, consistent tips. Or else, nobody would be a waiter/waitress at all (Which is actually what shitty chains with bad food/service like Applebees are now doing: nobody waits on you, you order from a tablet and then someone just delivers the food. Nobody even checks to see if the food is good or correct, lol).
