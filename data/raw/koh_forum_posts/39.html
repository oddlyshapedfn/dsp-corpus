          EDIT: Ideas To IMPROVE Prestreams (Per Poster Request!)

          in Suggestions

          Posted June 20, 2016

          1. Prestreams are the daily source for updates for those who tune into the streams but don't follow me on social media. Although I agree that it's probably not a huge group and a lot of the times they end up being the same info regurgitated or with only minor changes. But it's a necessary evil for those who aren't "on top of my shit" on a daily basis and let's be honest here, I'm not exactly the most important person on Earth and wouldn't expect everyone to always be fully informed.

          2. Prestreams allow me to make sure everything is working on YouTube. I couldn't tell you how many times an issue cropped up during these prestreams - fluctuating internet, YouTube f'ing up, me realizing that audio or something is off. Without a live audience, it's impossible to catch these issues by myself; I need the viewers to tell me when something is going on or wrong.

          3. Prestreams are the ONLY opportunity for me to advertise, at all, because I don't do plugs during gameplay streams. If there was no prestream, I'd get NO revenue from Amazon and nobody would know about Patreon etc. No prestream = significant dip in income for me.

          4. I like doing prestream. It prepares me to talk for 3.5 hours and I do get a bit of interaction with the chat from time to time. If I just went RIGHT into gameplay cold turkey it might end up feeling jarring or not natural.

          5. I'm not late for streams. I advertise that the stream will go live at 12pm PDT and it does go live before that time, almost every day unless there is a technical difficulty. The schedule doesn't say "GAMEPLAY STARTS AT NOON!" and anyone who assumes that puts that unfair expectation upon themselves. Everyone knows how my streaming works at this point so this isn't a valid criticism at all.

          I agree that tweaks can be made, but overall prestream does need to exist.
