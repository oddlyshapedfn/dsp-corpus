          Returning to Twitch? Thoughts and Discussion Thread

          in General

          Posted November 22, 2016

          SO today was certainly an interesting day.

          My OBS crashed, deleting ALL of my settings/scenes and forcing me to start from scratch. It'll take me a while to get back up to 100% as I've lost the templates for all of my shows and streams.

          However, I took this opportunity to try out OBS Studio, the latest software from the same people who made OBS. It pretty much does exactly the same things, only with one added ability: streaming in one quality, while simultaneously capturing in another.

          The entire reason I left Twitch was because I was going to be forced to stream at 3500kbps or leave the site. Being that, at the time in 2014, there wasn't a way to stream in one quality and record in another simultaneously (without multiple high-end PCs, which I do not have) and knowing that recording in 3500kbps would be FAR under what would be required for 1080p/60fps video, I opted to leave the streaming service.

          Sadly, even with initiatives like YouTube Gaming, YouTube streaming has NEVER taken off. I'm only getting my most "hardcore viewers" showing up for daily streaming, to the point where as of this week, I've decided not to stream at all, and cut back to only stream during game premieres and special events.

           

          For the last month, while asking for feedback regarding what I could do to improve my business, TONS of people have said "GO BACK TO TWITCH!" emphatically.

          Despite my moral disagreements with the website and business model as a whole, I'm considering returning to Twitch for two reasons:

          -It may help my business to continue, and I need all the help that I can get right now
          -RIDICULOUS amounts of people seem to want me to do it

           

          So, here are the pros and cons that I see:

          PROS:
          -Increased stream attendance (by default, since there's tons more people who are on the site regularly daily, and I'll actually show up in the search etc. while playing new releases)
          -"Improved" chat experience (which I disagree with, but it seems tons of people don't like the chat interface we use on YouTube)
          -POSSIBILITY of ad revenue and sub revenue if I can get back my partnered status
          -POSSIBILITY of people spilling over from my Twitch to my YouTube channel, ESPECIALLY if I don't live stream EVERYTHING as I've done in the past
          -I'd STILL be able to record at the same 1080/60fps quality as I do right now, so the YouTube videos on-demand should look the same
          -The new "Amazon/Twitch Prime" crossover program pretty much ensures I'd be getting some added subs if I can get that functionality enabled

          CONS:
          -I may not be able to get my old "DarksydePhil" channel and partnered status back, at all. Having to start completely over from scratch could be an uphill climb that's not winnable
          -I'd be limited to stream at 720p, period. The bandwidth limitations on Twitch make it impossible to stream at 1080p/60fps as I do now
          -Having more random stream attendees means a more unruly chat. Moderation would be a tougher job than it is now
          -My personal moral disagreements with the business make it a tough sell. Being on the same site where people make a living constantly shilling/begging and NOT putting out quality streams is hard to swallow. So is a business whose sole goal has been to find more ways to milk money out of viewers, rather than improve the quality of the overall streams. They've even changed their entire mission statement from being a premiere destination for gameplay streams, to "social interaction with the streamers." A fucking joke, from my perspective, and I'm not going to bend and conform to what they expect from a "moneymaking, profitable streamer."

          Keep in mind, if I do go back to Twitch, I am NOT going to change up my style. It'll still be focused primarily on gameplay, and I will NOT be interrupting that gameplay for "thank you shout-outs" and all of the other pandering strategies that make other streamers tons of money, for all of the WRONG reasons.

           

           

          What I'd like: to hear YOUR take. With SO MANY saying moving back to Twitch is the right thing to do, I'd definitely appreciate your feedback.

          What do you guys think? Might be a good initiative for next year.

          Green screen can definitely come back now with no problems. I'll be thinking about ways to implement it soon...

          Fan submitted content is a VERY slippery slope. Despite YouTUbe's policy being BETTER, it would be VERY hard to justify uploading someone else's work to my channel and bringing in ad revenue for it. I honestly don't even know what the rules on YouTube are for that kind of thing, and again, if somehow profit needs to be shared, I'm not even sure how to do that. YouTube's analytics aren't very accurate at all, nor are they timely (sometimes they freeze for weeks at a time). If people were willing to submit stuff JUST to get exposure, and not caring about the money, then it would be a far more reasonable thing....but then again, someone COULD attempt to claim copyright as the original owner of that video too. It's a very gray area.
