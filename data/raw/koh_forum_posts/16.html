          The fact that I've lived in Washington for over 3 years and had this internet setup the same way the entire time, but people STILL don't understand how it works, baffles me.

          I have two internet lines.

          One is a highest level available of business package from Comcast, and it is in the office. This line is for:

          -Live Streaming
          -Uploading to YouTube
          -Playing games online

          NOTE: I NEVER upload and live stream at the same time, nor do I upload and play MP games at the same time. When uploading, I'm not using the internet for ANYTHING else.

           

          I have a second, much slower internet line downstairs, to which all of my wireless devices (phone, laptop, TV for Amazon Prime/WWE Network etc.) are connected. The reason: having these devices also connected to the business line would dramatically reduce speeds, so I purposely have the two lines so NOTHING hinders the business line.

          In addition, having the second line allows for much more security with the business line; since ALL I do is stream/upload/play MP games on it, there's far less chance the IP could be discovered leading to DDOS attacks and the like.
