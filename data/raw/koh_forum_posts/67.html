          Is having stream chat help a good thing? Or does it ruin the experience?

          in Suggestions

          Posted February 15, 2015

          The problem is, there's zero agreement on this issue, which makes it hard for me to have any determination on what to do.

          On one side: people think I should interact with stream chat MORE, that I ignore people and don't respond to them/talk to them much or at all, and therefore it's not an interactive experience. Some people have even questioned why I stream because I don't interact with the stream chat enough (which I don't understand as I thought the point of streaming was to see whatever event you're watching live, NOT to talk to or interact with a streamer constantly, but that's just me). And for games where things aren't easily explained or I'm trying out for the first time without ANY prior knowledge of the series (like Monster Hunter today) that I should be looking/asking for tips from the chat, since the viewers probably have that knowledge. I will be honest: without help from stream chat, finishing up Dark Souls 1, completing a few very cryptic puzzles in Grim Fandango,  and probably even trying Monster Hunter 4 Ultimate wouldn't have happened. In addition, many people feel that when I get "stuck" somewhere in a game that it's boring/repetitive and makes people want to stop watching, even if I rage (some HATE when I do so).

          On the flip side: people think I shouldn't interact with or even LOOK at the stream chat at all, because I started out as a YouTube "Let's Player" and never had that element to my playthroughs until 2013, when I started streaming. People feel that 1. my addressing things in the chat takes AWAY from the on-demand experience of YouTube because it's distracting from the game and 2. that getting help from the chat is "a crutch" that lessens the experience of the playthrough. Some even specifically address moments of repeated fails/rage as HIGHLIGHTS of my playthrough and say that if I get my "hand held" through a tough spot that it takes away from the gameplay.

           

          So there you have it: two completely different opinions both with some pretty valid justifications for/against stream chat interaction. I'll be darned if I know what the proper balance is, but here's my personal opinion:

          -It's okay to get help from the stream chat for games that are 1. cryptic or 2. don't explain things well/expect you to have previous experience in a series. 
          -It's not okay to have the stream chat constantly hold the gamer's hand and tell them how to do EVERYTHING in a game
          -Some/limited stream chat interaction is good (referring to a funny joke or thanking someone for a very helpful and NEEDED tip) 
          -Constant stream chat interaction is bad (stopping constantly to talk directly to certain individuals, or "shout outs" to things said in chat, or interruption of the gameplay experience constantly"

          I try to use the "happy medium" policy in my playthroughs but sometimes it doesn't always work as planned. And since there's no 100% right/wrong answer, there's always going to be people who feel disappointed at how a certain playthrough turns out. That's life.
