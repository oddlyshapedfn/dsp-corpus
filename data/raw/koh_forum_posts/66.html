          A question regarding playthroughs and commentary

          in General

          Posted February 19, 2015

          It's a double-edged sword bc not everyone looks for the same things in a playthrough w/commentary.

          There are lots of Let's Players who literally use their gameplay video as a way to vlog - I've seen FPS/COD gamers who put up videos of a match and talk over it about their day, or politics, or whatever. Or do the same during grinding/boring parts of a video game playthrough. That's a perfectlu valid "style" of doing commentary.

          My style has always been reactionary: I play the game, focus on the game, and react to it. I may make observational jokes based on what's going on, and they may have some kind of outside reference to world news/pop culture, but that's it. You won't expect to see me ranting about the latest meme or trend in a gameplay video, because that's not what I do. I personally feel that detracts from the gameplay - people are tuning in to watch me play the game, not hear me vent on stuff.

          That's why I have a vlogging channel - where I can do a podcast/week in preview/ask the king and actually share my feelings OUTSIDE of the gameplay on everything from news to personal experiences and the like. There's a place for commentary unrelated to the gameplay, and that's it.

          Talking to the chat is another issue entirely. I DO sometimes reference the chat if 1. an on-topic joke is made and I find it funny or 2. they directly help me in a time when I'm completely or utterly stuck. But no, I won't engage in a chat conversation during gameplay because, at heart, I'm a YouTube Let's Player/Gamer and I didn't HAVE the chat when I started in 2008. The majority of my viewing audience (for the most part w/few exceptions) is the "on-demand" viewers, not the live stream chat. They want an uninterrupted experience that feels like I'm focused on the game, because they're not there to share in the stream chat experience. Imagine if you were watching a movie, but every 3 minutes the main actor turned to one of the crew and made reference to inside jokes or things only they could hear? I'd be incredibly distracting and frustrating.

          However, chat-reactionary commentary style DOES WORK and IS VALID for those who PRIMARILY stream and focus on that experience. As in, people who tune in to see their favorite "personality" play games - not see a legitimate, unhindered gameplay experience. They're there because they're PRIMARILY attracted to the person and don't care about the actual game being played; there's an entire MARKET for this on Twitch, leading to people constantly giving shout-outs for donations, etc. That's not where I come from, that's not why I game, and that's not what I want to do.

           

          I could easily turn what I do into the "hang with DSP while he plays games" stream instead of the "serious/fun game playthrough session" but it would isolate the audience that's been with me from the beginning. I would probably get more stream attendance and lower views overall on the on-demand stuff. Which is better?
