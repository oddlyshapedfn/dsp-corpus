          Have Phil's feelings on Minecraft really changed?

          in General

          Posted March 3, 2015

          Without EVER having played Minecraft...

          From what I know of the game, it appears to be one of those "no real end goal, time waster because it's incredibly addictive" kind of games.

          It is a "bad" game? Not necessarily and there's obviously a market for it, since you know, it's made BILLIONS of dollars.

          But it is a pretty DUMB game. It's simple, it's addictive, it's only REALLY fun once you mod the shit out of it to look like stuff it wasn't intended to be originally. It's purposely got some of the worst graphics on the planet so that the most amount of people can play it regardless of level of PC or platform (it was designed that way). Notch KNEW what he was doing: designing an incredibly silly, stupid, dumb, BASIC, but addictive and fun game.

           

          What issue do I have with the game?

           

          Game devs put YEARS of work, voice acting, graphical/art/level design, painstaking attention to detail and voice acting etc. - making major artistic masterpieces that have pushed gaming into the realm of true art in the past 5+ years. Nobody can doubt that.

          But what does everyone know? Minecraft. What does every "non-hardcore gamer" play? Minecraft. What has made the most money despite being a literal throw-away experience? MINECRAFT.

          Ask anyone WHY Minecraft is such a great game, and I doubt they could quantify it to the point of explaining why it's so much better than other games. In fact, I think lots of people wouldn't even SAY it's better than other games. Yet it's the game MOST PEOPLE ARE PLAYING/HAVE PLAYED.

           

          Equate this to the Nintendo Wii's success, where the integration of idiotic motion controls turned into MILLIONS for years and years of sales. Hardcore gamers knew it was a waste of time and just a gimmick, but to those who weren't into games, it was LOVE! As if gaming had nothing to offer but waving your arms around like an idiot in front of your TV. But that was the mainstream gaming fad - until MINECRAFT came along, that is.

          And that's my best explanation: I dislike Minecraft for being a popular FAD, not a popular game. The game itself does nothing wrong, but when it makes billions of dollars and other games that are infinitely better, have tons more time/effort/artistic value put into them get IGNORED for this?! Logic has to come into play somewhere and it just doesn't in this equation. And that's the world we live in.

           

          This Friday, I'll see for the first time, firsthand, what this fad is all about.

          A look at Phils usage of the term business

          in General

          Posted March 3, 2015

          Off-topic, derailing and insulting posts removed and one user perm banned. Why the immature have to bring personal insults, opinions and derailing negativity into an objective thread like this is beyond me. But those people are making their exit one by one, so it's all good.

          People seem to be confusing "professional" with "competitive." Professional means "makes a living doing something" while competitive means "can beat out others in some sort of competition." 

          There's literally nothing whatsoever regarding "skill level" involved in being a "professional" at something. It's a misuse of the word in common language that's led to TONS of misunderstandings and misconceptions. Which, again, people saying they were "professional fighting game players" before the release of Street Fighter 4 were always factually wrong because prize pots weren't enough to make a living, even if you won every tournament in the country in a year.

          Therefore, by the definition of the word:

          I am a professional gamer.

          I am a professional commentator.

          I am a professional YouTuber.

          I am a professional entertainer.

           

          Since I make my living (financial income) doing all four four of the above, that is what I'd be considered a professional of. It doesn't mean I'm the best at it (which is subjective anyway) or that I put any more/less work/effort/skill/talent into it than anyone else. It just means I make a living doing it.
