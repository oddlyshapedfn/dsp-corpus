          in Archive

          Posted March 13, 2015

          The game just started, you just met the characters, there's no dialogue, zero emotional investment and one of them "dies" despite being obviously overweight. JUST BY LOOKING AT HIM you know he couldn't starve to death. It's a pretty odd design choice and no, I didn't feel anything. Compare that to Valiant Hearts, Brothers: A Tale of Two Sons and other indie games with far better writing, and that actually made sense.

          Maybe I don't allow myself to be overly emotionally touched by simple stuff like this? Kissing a game's butt because it's trying to put emotion into a generic platformer doesn't fly with me.

          And sadly, after 2 hours that's exactly what the game is turning into: generic Metroid-Vania style gameplay. Child of Light had that last year, and also had the same amazing-style visuals, but it also had turn-based RPG gameplay, actual dialogue and a large cast of characters. By the end of the Ori stream, half the viewers had left stating they'd gotten bored to death. And honestly? I saw nothing really original about the game at all. Pretty standard stuff.

          Hate to say it but this may be another case of reviewers blowing smoke up everyone's butt just because a $20 game is "purdy" and actually tried to put some emotion into the start of the game.
