          Thoughts on Phil's May 2016 Patreon Goal Event

          in General

          Posted May 16, 2016

          Seems that all of the criticisms of my event in this thread are erroneous, because they're based on misinformation. In fact, the title of THIS VERY THREAD is erroneous and I'm going to change it to avoid further misinformation.

          This event is not fan appreciation. Nowhere in my announcement video or on Patreon does it say "fan appreciation." I even say in the video "don't worry, the free multiplayer events (i.e. fan appreciation) aren't going away." It's an attempt to have a controlled environment to play multiplayer w/my Patrons. If you pledge $5 in May, and enough people pledge, everyone who wants to will be able to play with me, troll-free (or, if someone is actually stupid enough to pay and then troll, it'll last about 4 minutes until they're all banned/removed from the sessions permanently so the rest of us can play issue-free). This is NOT unpredecented (tons of people do it with subs/Patrons and the like) and I actually got the idea from a few other YouTubers, who will remain nameless lest someone accuse me of "stealing someone else's idea!" and trying to start shit with them. But anyway, anyone saying "you can't call it fan appreciation and charge for it!" is correct; that's why I DIDN'T CALL IT THAT. So anyone who continues to say I did, will appropriately be corrected.

          This event is NOT US-only. What I stated in the video is that I cannot be held responsible for those who may pledge, but we can't get our connections to work due to issues such as being international to the USA. Case in point: there have been a few people who've wanted to play me in SF for years, but every time they've tried (across multiple games) the connection is god awful and either won't connect or lags out constantly. I have zero control over circumstances like that, so I obviously can't be held accountable for it, but I WILL at the very least give it a shot. This isn't limited to SF either; I've had people trying to join my games and get errors tons of times over the years. It's just a symptom of not having equivalent internet worldwide yet. 

          Lastly, it seems there are concerns about scheduling. I'm not setting a date for when the event is going to happen yet for two reasons:
          -We don't even know if it's happening yet; it'd be pointless to set a date before we know if the funding level is hit. It would just get peoples' hopes up for something that may or may not happen.
          -Once the funding is hit, one major part of the thread to say which games you'd like to play wil also be a poll to "pick the best date" for your participation. Based on the polling, that's when the event will be. If a bunch of people can't make the date, I'd be willing to do a second day as a make-up. Like I said in the video, I REALLY want this event to be successful as it's a totally new concept and I want to play MP with viewers WITHOUT issues, for a change.

           

          Okay. Any other concerns, feel free to address, but please stop spreading misinformation about this event when it seems the biggest "issues" aren't issues at all, just based on total misconceptions.

          To Phil - An observation on the negativity and how to deal with it

          in Suggestions

          Posted May 8, 2016

          If everyone just treated everyone else with respect, and had an attitude of "let's enjoy Phil's videos" instead of "let's attack/defend Phil constantly," I think things would be a lot better overall for everyone.

          When I was super popular on YouTube and things were great (i.e. tons of views, tons of money and everyone looked at me in a mostly positive light) the attitudes of viewers were FAR different. Were there people who disliked me, to the point of obsession? YES, but they were laughed off (remember NaughtyNightwear, THEKINGOFWEIGHTHD and DSPCultLeader? Yeah, and those were around 2010-11 when I was at the HEIGHT of my popularity) and nobody took them seriously.

          It wasn't until TIHYDP, my business started seeing declines, I panicked and reacted badly to most of the criticisms etc. circa 2012 that things flipped, from what I can see. Instead of seeing me as "the average guy that kind of sucks, but enjoys games and we like watching him because he's normal like us," it became "that guy who never admits fault, blames everything but himself and doesn't deserve any kind of success, at all, so FUCK HIM!" I was quick to try and blame drops in viewership on everything but myself, while it was obvious that online gaming was changing (direct capture, live streaming etc.) and I was behind the times. I didn't HELP that people were constantly insulting me and talking shit; it just added to the whole negativity of the thing and it made me react badly. I'd say the blame is 50/50 split on others and myself for how it all went down.

          Now, over the years of constant attacks and negativity, we have three groups of people:

          -Those who outright hate me for various (crazy) reasons and will do anything to see me fail
          -Those who still watch my stuff despite knowing I'm NOT a perfect person; they roll with the punches, the highs/lows and sometimes love my videos, sometimes hate them depending on what's going on
          -Those who watch ALL of my stuff, staunchly defend it all and think I'm mostly above criticism, with a few exceptions

          I honestly don't know how to fix this as it's all new to me. This has been a wild 7.5-year ride that's still going, and it's ALL a learning experience. All I think I can do, is to keep trying to stay positive and have a great attitude about stuff.

          Lack of Creativity on KOGaming?

          in General

          Posted May 5, 2016

          Already kind of explained this....

          The idea for KOGaming is edited content, mixed in with the daily streaming/raw gameplay on DSPGaming. What you see on that channel is directly related to what I'm playing, because I need to create the raw footage to use in edited content, or else there's nothing to edit together.

          People have expressed interest in countdowns: so have I. But right now gaming is VERY busy with new releases and ongoing projects (Persona 3, for example, which needs to continue but has been on hold) so I don't have any time to work on them. I foresee this Summer, that changing in a big way, as there aren't many games slated for release at ALL. That would be the perfect time to "scour the archives" and work on some countdown series.

          I'll be frank, because I always am: Things are good right now business-wise. March/April have been far better than January/February were when it comes to what I earned on YouTube, and if I can keep that up for the rest of the year, I think I'll be able to keep the business afloat and do this long-term (which I was very afraid of losing the ability to do, after my contract renegotiations in late 2015 with Machinima). To do that, I have to keep at it, keep playing a wide variety of games, keep streaming daily and keeping people engaged. Taking time out to do edited content isn't going to work well with that formula, unless that content is directly related to what's going on over on DSPGaming (such as 1st impressions, reviews etc.).

          In January/February I focused HEAVILY on edited content: my year-end series, Hits of the Week and the like. What happened? After an initial rush of interest, views tanked despite people telling me I was actually IMPROVING with each video. I was spending upwards of 6+ hours on a single edited video, that would get 3-4k views; FYI, that's about 5 dollars of profit. Does it make sense to work 6 hours and make $5? That's less than a dollar an hour...

          ...OR I could focus more on what people are liking/watching more: streams, gameplay, and vlogs. Admittedly even my vlogging has suffered a bit in the past month, but it'll pick up again this week with my thoughts on Civil War and hopefully a few news stories this month, or something to that effect. But to take my focus away from what people are watching and work more on what people are watching less and less? How does that make any sense?

           

          If people have ideas for KOGaming, by all means, please share them. But understand that my hands are tied when it comes to what I work on daily, because I have to do what's paying the bills. Edited content MAY be what's popular for other YouTubers, but when it comes to those who watch my videos, it seems that the better I've gotten/the MORE edited content I put out, the less people watch overall. So that's saying something, and I have to LISTEN to that and react to it or else I'll quickly find myself OUT of business.

          NO, I actually meant imagine playing Dark Souls in reverse. You're one of the OP bosses, infinite stamina and can do ridiculous attack patterns vs. everything else in the game. BUT you have to traverse the same kind of perilous ledges etc. as the normal guy. So it ends up being quite difficult because, even though you're pretty much a godlike tank, mobs can still "mob" you, your attacks can miss (how many bosses whiff against you?) etc. Plus you can easily overswing, get stuck/fall off ledges etc. to your doom.
