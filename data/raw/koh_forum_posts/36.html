          What changes did YouTube make to screw over the little guy?

          -Engagement rate giving priority to videos (videos that are watched for a longer % of time show up more often/higher in search), but due to the nature of their flawed algorithm, it gives longer videos FAR more priority than shorter ones (Which is why everyone's Let's Plays went from 10 minute clips to 30+ minutes within a year). I had HUNDREDS of thousands of viewers who checked out my channel page daily but didn't subscribe, because YouTube NEVER optimized viewers' subscription boxes. Tons of people watched my stuff but never subbed, to avoid sub box spam from my videos, so my subscription count remained relatively low. It's incredibly ridiculous that there are so many people out there with 1 million + subscribers, a "Golden Play Button" to celebrate the achievement, but they don't have as many views on their channels as I do on DSPGaming. It just goes to show COMPLETELY lack of understanding on Google's part as to how YouTube works, and what actually matters.

           

          In short: all of the practices that got me popular from 2009-2012 were completely undone by Google, who changed YouTube completely and didn't tell anyone but their "inner circle" of YouTubers. Because 1. I wasn't in-the-know on what was going on and 2. I wanted to remain true to my core viewers, I got completely fucked. And sadly, adopting the tactics of these successful guys now would not work for me. I'm perpetually stuck where I am, with DSPGaming, at least.
