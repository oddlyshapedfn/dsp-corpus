          Marvel vs Capcom Infinite - How long will Phil play it?

          in General

          Posted September 28, 2017

          2 hours ago, moka'roka said:

          I heard Phil mention some one was doing a 50/50.  But his explaination of what it was differs from what i thought it was.  

          A 50/50, in laymans terms, means doing something to confuse your opponent so they don't know how to block. The "50/50" term refers to a 50% of needing to block high, and a 50% chance of needing to block low.

          In most fighting games, there is a risk/reward factor involved: you risk leaving yourself open to punishment if the opponent reads your 50/50 attempt, defends properly and then counterattacks; this is how it's SUPPOSED TO BE as it creates an element of skill.

          The problem with the 50/50s in MvC Infinite is that MOST OF THEM are completely safe.  In MvC Infinite, the 50/50s are literally mindless: like continuously doing a horizontal dash while mashing Fierce Punch randomly, making it so it's almost impossible to see when to block it. There's also the factor of online play: characters like Spider-Man are ALMOST IMPOSSIBLE to defend against their 50/50s because the frames of animation don't even completely come out! The game's netcode skips entire frames and therefore you can't "read" how you're supposed to block at all, making the game completely and utterly unfair for those who do NOT have those insanely safe 50/50 mixups. And of course, you can tag in/out almost an ANY time, meaning that if your 50/50 IS actually defended against properly and you don't have a character whose 50/50 attempt is safe, you can tag in to MAKE it safe. This completely undermines the entire philosophy of the 50/50 in the first place, making the game watered-down, safe mode drivel instead of something that actually takes skill to play.

          And that's why I complain about the game so much. It was made incredibly easy to play, and dumbed down to the point of mediocrity. The amazing-looking combos, mixups and 50/50s in Marvel vs. Capcom 1 and 2 were actually difficult to perform; in MvC Infinite, they're MEANT to be incredibly easy to execute, making the game a mundane mess.
