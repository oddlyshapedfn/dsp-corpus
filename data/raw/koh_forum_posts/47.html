IN FACT, I'd go as far to say that #3 is the entire reason the genre even exists right now: game devs have figured out these games are cash cows in the long run.

          Sadly, TONS of gamers don't have the "big picture" view to see that these games aren't anything significant. The games that these new team MMO-shooters take from DEFINED genres and pop culture for a while (i.e. Halo and Team Fortress 2) while these "new" games just lift things from them, in the disguise of something "new and fresh" when in reality, you're just playing the same game again in a team-based co-op setting, and paying over and over AND OVER for it.

          However, as we've seen in the past, these things tend to kill themselves. Fighting games became SO oversaturated in the late 90s/early 2000s that they all but died out for the greater part of that decade, ONLY getting a resurgence with SF4 in 2007. Music-style games flooded the market with so many peripherals and pseudo-sequel expansion packs that the market completely fell out from under them. FPS games are few and far between today besides a few established franchises (Battlefield/COD/Halo etc.).

          And I greatly suspect the same exact thing will happen to these team-based co-op/MMO shooters: there will end up being 10 of them in a year (maybe this year, actually) and there will be NO WAY that anyone could possibly play them all. The market will fall out because too many people will be pulled in too many directions to financially support ALL of their ongoing existence.

          And sadly? Nothing will be lost. Almost NONE of these games give any kind of a considerable narrative experience; it's just about the repetitive, grinding gameplay that somehow feels better when you do it with friends. You can literally skip every one of them and you miss nothing. And THAT'S the problem I have with the genre as a whole: NOBODY IS FUCKING TRYING! They just rehash and realize that little work = big payouts, and I'm sick of it. We are in a console generation where game devs should be making artistic MASTERPIECES, and instead everyone is all up-in-a-tizzy about the latest "grind til you drop" piece of boring, repetitive shit made SOLELY to make money, not create a worthwhile gaming experience. People need to wake up.
