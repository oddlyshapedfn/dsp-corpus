          Why VR is a thing

          in General

          Posted October 15, 2016

          10 hours ago, markymark52 said:

          1. Eye Strain and Fatigue - After only a half hour playing EVE: Valkyrie, I experienced noticeable eye strain after I removed the headset. While it was overall mild, it still was bothersome and caused me to rub my eyes a few times the next day to relieve some of the pressure I felt. I can't imagine using the device for lengthy gaming sessions between 4-5 hours, even with breaks. Additionally, sweat accumulates on your forehead and back of the head over time and the pressure from the device will make your face feel fatigued. These issues may go away after I get more used to the device, but how many people will have the patience to let themselves adapt to the device?

          2. Short Games - Due to the issue above, I believe the vast majority of the games developed for the device will be short experiences. Most people won't have the patience to deal with the headset after a couple of hours, and continuing a lengthy game day after day will end up being more of a chore due to the bulky and awkward nature of the device.

          3. 1st Generation VR - While the experience is awesome, the demos and games I've seen have significant blurriness (lower resolution) with severe aliasing compared to what you experience with a "regular" PS4 game. Furthermore, The overall detail in VR games due seem to be significantly less in comparison to what you'd get in a "regular" PS4 game (sparks in VR are more pixexlated in Drive Club VR for example). Unfortunately, this can be a distraction and really shows how underpowered the base console is with the VR technology.

          My feedback/response:

          I'm PURPOSELY not playing anything for longer than, say, 15-20 minutes at a time to prevent eye strain and headaches. In addition, I seem to need a break regardless because the sides of my nose/forehead are incredibly sweaty after that period of time, and sometimes smudging occurs on the lenses as well. Ultimately the PSVR is going to work amazingly for those who have the "ideal face" for it and it'll work slightly less effectively for others, I think.

          But yeah, you've hit the nail right on the head: how can you make a "long-form" game for PSVR, or any VR, when the headsets become uncomfortable after 20 minutes or so? It's impossible. You have to make games that are REALLY digestible in smaller chapters, and certain genres just aren't going to work properly like that. Right now, I'm having a blast playing all of these "short" games but after this week, I bet the content will dry up for QUITE a while.

          And Resident Evil 7? I'd love to play it in VR, but realistically I don't think it's going to be feasible. At least, not with this headset where I need breaks so frequently. Imagine trying to beat a 12+ hour survival horror game, with cheap deaths and cryptic puzzles, all while your head is sweating and your eyes are throbbing. Not gonna happen, lol!
