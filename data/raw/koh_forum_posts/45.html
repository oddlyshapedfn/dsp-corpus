          Rolling over isn't possible and makes no sense. The objective of Patreon is monthly fundraising; if I rolled over, then there would be little motivation for anyone new to pledge in April, and I'd probably see a huge drop in funding for the month.           People seem to have a misconception that Patreon is like Kickstarter; it's not, AT ALL. The business models are completely different: on Kickstarter, you're funding a project with the promise of a final product to be produced. With Patreon, you're directly funding ongoing effort of someone you enjoy, no matter what they create. Essentially, as long as they don't "cut and run" (i.e. do a money grab, then immediately cease all activities for what the pledges were for) they're living up to their end of the bargain, regardless if they concretely produce a product or not.

          Key difference: Kickstarter is a way to raise funds to produce a product, in the hopes of selling that product and making profit on it. While Patreon is just direct financial support of someone, in the hopes they can "keep doing what they're doing" because you find it entertaining/worthwhile. Many people who use Patreon use it as their SOLE source of income. Luckily for me I also have my YouTube partnership, because there's no way I'd be able to do this as a job only bringing in $1250 a month on Patreon.
