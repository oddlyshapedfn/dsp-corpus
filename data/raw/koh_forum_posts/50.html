          Disscussion on Fine Bros, and the Quick panic of people reactions

          in Other

          Posted January 31, 2016

          Actually here are the words of the actual trademark attempt, which is in progress:

          "

          For:
          Entertainment services, namely, providing an on-going series of programs and webisodes via the Internet in the field of observing and interviewing various groups of people

           

          SO they are attempting to trademark any video in which someone is being directly observed reacting to something/being interviewed while reacting to something WHICH - and this is important - has the word REACT in the title. If it doesn't call itself "REACT" at all, they'd have no power over it.

          If this trademark goes through and I wanted to record myself live reacting to E3? If I titled it "Phil Reacts to E3" I couldn't do it. If I titled it "Phil Responds to E3" or "Phil Watches E3 Live" it would be fine, because the word React isn't in it. They couldn't actually trademark/copyright the ACT of me reacting, but they could force me to call it something else.

          However, as for my vlogging called "Phil Reacts?" It's not about observing or interviewing people, at all! It's just me giving you my thoughts on something, not even watching it. So it's not the same thing and they wouldn't have a legal leg to stand on.

           

          The one thing everyone is forgetting here: the Fine Bros. can't do anything without a lawsuit. YouTube REFUSES to PERMANENTLY take down any video unless someone gets sued. So in regard to the above where someone said that there was a series of videos called "Seniors React" and the Fine Bros. had it taken down? The only way they could do so would be to SUE the makers of those videos in a massive lawsuit. I don't believe they did that, at ALL; I think that the creators of "Seniors React" didn't educate themselves and just took down the videos instead of counter-claiming them on YouTube after the initial DMCA takedown notices hit.

          I'd love to make a video explaining all this, but with the Rock-A-THon tomorrow, my personal day on Monday and the Black Ops III DLC covering Tuesday/Wednesday, I wouldn't even be able to touch the subject until Thursday...

Between losing thousands of dollars from Sept-November of 2015 due to false copyright strikes, paired with my contract renegotiation for MUCH less w/Machinima, I'm completely financially strapped and cannot take on any additional costs. Simply put, I couldn't afford to do a giveaway in 2015 because the shipping costs are astronomical, due to my worldwide fanbase.

          To give some perspective:
          -Shipping T-shirts for the $30 Patron perk frequently cost as much or MORE than the $30 pledge. I did the math in December and realized I'd actually LOST MONEY on the shirts overall! Which is why they were removed as a perk in 2016, and how absolutely ridiculous it is that idiots seem to think I owe anyone money for them, when I've never made a cent of profit on their sale (including at conventions in 2012, which never even made up for half the amount of money I'd initially invested into them!).           -In 2014, when I was mailing out all of the rewards for that year's Hardcore Gaming Season Giveaway, I spent over $600 just through the month of November! Again, things have changed and I can't take on hundreds of dollars in losses anymore.
