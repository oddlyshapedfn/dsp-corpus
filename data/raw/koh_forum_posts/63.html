          Something doesn't make sense to me regarding Phil's upload method for the online matches. Why upload a million 2-3 minute videos?  To me it makes more sense to take all the matches for each individual character and combine them into one video. For example,  take all the matches he played as Sub-Zero this session and put them in one video then upload that doing the same for the other characters he played.  Maybe that's just me though. 

          ​This method makes zero sense for the viewer, but makes tons of sense for the uploader who wants to take advantage of/abuse YouTube's now prevalent "the longer the video, the better it shows up in searches" filtering (which is utter BS).

          If i edited all of today's Sub-Zero matches into a 35-minute montage, how would you know where you left off if you stop watching? What if one of those matches is awesome and you want to keep rewatching/referencing it later, but you have to fast forward through 24 minutes before you even get to it? It's INCREDIBLY annoying to do it this way.

          Compare that with how I uploaded today: numbered and named each video in a detailed manner so that, let's say, you want to watch me beat that guy with almost 300 wins again. You can IMMEDIATELY find the video and watch it instead of having to remember who I played with, what part of a 35 minute montage it's at, etc.

          The ONLY detriment to a viewer for me uploading separate matches is that they'll get 20 videos in their video inbox. This can be remedied by telling YouTube you don't want every single video of an uploader to show up in your inbox (this feature has been there for THREE years now) but most YouTube viewers don't use it, but hold it against me that they don't.

           

          HOWEVER - why do most "successful gaming YouTubers" now upload montage-style clips of fighting games? Why do they record 30-minute+ clips of their raw playthroughs? It has nothing to do with accessibility for the viewers, at all. 

          Answer: in 2012 YouTube changed the way the site worked (without telling anyone) so that longer videos which get longer engagement (the amount of time someone watches them) get MASSIVE priority in search engines, being featured on the sides of other videos, etc. So despite the fact that my uploading 20 individual matches today massively benefits the viewers AND makes it easier for me to just get my content up ASAP - YouTube PUNISHES ME for having short videos. Those videos will NOT show up in common search or related videos because EVERYBODY now abuses the "make a 30 minute montage to get more video engagement" method to get their stuff shoved to the forefront. Then in early 2013 YouTube RESET the entire search engine so previously popular videos wouldn't show up anymore unless they fell under the new "engagement" criteria; so for example, my Spider-Man Web of Shadows/Shattered Dimensions playthroughs for which parts had MILLIONS of views and are the highest-viewed playthroughs of those games ever DON'T EVEN SHOW UP IN SEARCH ANYMORE because the videos are around 10 minutes long, instead of 30. BRILLIANT!

          I'm about efficiency and what's best for my viewers. Being able to find what you want to watch ASAP is better than me getting extra views off of YOuTube's insanely stupid, broken, and abusable search system. So single matches will be uploaded so you can see my content as soon as possible and I'll do my best to put good descriptions for the matches you'll want to see again.
