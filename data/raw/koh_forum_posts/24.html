          KOGaming: Year One

          in General

          Posted January 27, 2017

          My biggest challenge last year was maintaining a balance. Remember I had a three-pronged plan:

          -Gameplay on DSPGaming

          -Edited content on KOGaming

          -Vlogging on THEKINGOFHATEVLOGS

          When gaming was slow, it was easy to maintain the balance because I would take the 2nd half of every single day to work on either a vlog or edited video.

          But as SOON as things got busy, notably from March-May and then again from September-December, I literally found myself with no time to do much at all besides 1. play the new games and 2. review them. If I didn't end up playing double sessions, I fell far behind on certain games, leading to viewers getting frustrating and moving on. If I focused only on the games, then I fell behind on edited content/vlogging and people moved on from KOGaming altogether.


          This is not easy. I'm really enjoying making the edited style content, but it takes a LOT more time and effort to produce it than just playing games raw on DSPGaming. And it's a double-edged sword, because when some videos work out well (Homefront, or the coverage of No Man's Sky that I did during the summer that got outstanding views) then it's TOTALLY worth it. But take a look at the videos since then: only 2 videos after August 2016 even topped 20k total views, and my year-end series? They didn't perform anywhere nearly as well as they usually do, due to being on KOGaming instead of DSPGaming. And those took a TON of time to put together.

           

          I REALLY want to keep putting out content for KOGaming; at the VERY least I'd like to put out at least one meaningful video a week there. But it's going to be tough with the new releases I'm covering, unless I cut back; and when I cut back, that's less gameplay for DSPGaming and therefore, less income. The problem I've seen is that it seems people ONLY want to show up when I make a negative video; if a game is great, I'm not going to lie, nitpick or just be an angry dick to get some kind of rise out of a viewing audience (Despite some YouTubers being OVERLY angry as their schtick, that's just not me). So I got a ton of attention for the Homefront and NMS negative vids, but as soon as I started legitimately reviewing other stuff, people seemed to lose interest and move on.

          Without being negative ALL THE TIME, I have no idea how to retain YouTube viewership on KOGaming. It seems that negativity is all that breeds on the website now, and it's pretty sickening to think about that. If I were a raging, swearing, insulting, negative-centric asshole that just bashed everything I played, insulted everyone else and created drama, I'd probably be a millionaire. There are people who ONLY put out that content that are 10 times bigger than me. But that's not me, so I don't know what can be done.
