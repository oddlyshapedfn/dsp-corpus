          in General

          Posted March 11, 2018

          There is no legal recourse for anything that has been done against me, because most of the really hurtful stuff (DOXXing/SWATing/False Copyright Strikes etc.) were done anonymously and there's absolutely no way to prove that any one individual did any of it.

          As for the accounts directly referenced who slander me on a regular basis, as much as it actually WOULD probably help me to prevent them from being slanderous in the future, there is nothing legally that can be done against them. It is YouTube's fault that their toxic content is allowed to be posted, because ultimately they are responsible for what is posted up on their own website/business. And as we all know, using the "tools" the put into place (flagging videos for harmful/slanderous content/bullying, or copyright strike takedowns) doesn't work, AT ALL. I find it hilarious that idiots still to this day accuse me of trying to take down videos using these methods, because it's been factually proven that they are broken, automated methods that don't actually review the content subjectively and if no personal PHYSICAL threats are put out on YouTube, nothing will ever be shut down. And you can only ever permanently take down a video by filing a lawsuit PER INDIVIDUAL VIDEO, making it financially impossible to ever do anything effective. I've never flagged a video because I know it doesn't work. I talked with YouTube personally about this years ago and confirmed they, simply put, won't do a damn thing about anything unless they feel they'll get sued over it.

          In summary: YouTube is a website that 100% fully allows people to post up slanderous content on an unlimited basis, for free, but they do NOT allow people to defend themselves against said content. It's completely illegal, but until someone creates a huge class-action lawsuit (which could potentially amount to the hundreds of millions in damages for reputations and the like that have been ruined by the site), nothing will ever be done. And I'm certainly not the person to do it: I'm small potatoes in comparison to much bigger, more popular personalities who have been torn apart by slander on the site.

          So as much as I appreciate that someone might care enough, don't waste your breath on this.

          Welcome back to Twitch DSP

          in General

          Posted February 28, 2018

          Basically, the rule of thumb is this: you can thank people for cheering, you can give them recognition for top cheer, but you can't ever ask for cheers, period, or allude to it being currency at all. That means no cheer/bit goals, and no saying what you put anything you raise via cheers towards (so saying "I'm raising funds for this reason and your cheers help" is 100% not allowed). You can't allude that cheers help you in any way, you can't say that you need people to be supportive in general terms - you HAVE to be specific and call out subs/tips so that cheers are left out. 

          So doing a stream with sub/tip goals, is 100% fine. ASKING for subs and tips is totally fine. But having cheer goals, or asking for cheers (even if you don't necessarily say "GIVE ME BITS" but instead just say "come to my stream and be supportive!") or treating them as currency isn't allowed. You HAVE to make it so that cheers are totally left out of the equation, at all times.
