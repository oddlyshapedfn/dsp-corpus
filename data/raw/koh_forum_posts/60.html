          Witcher 3 Playthrough Discussion

          in Playthroughs

          Posted June 3, 2015

          First, I've had zero challenge at all in the combat of the game up to that optional hunt, so this was really the first battle where the combat engine was put to the test. You can literally steamroll EVERYTHING if you just use basic tactics (like using Quen/mind control/etc during fights) instead of just rushing it. It's not complex at all, it's incredibly basic for an RPG and it works 95% of the time UNLESS you run into something far above your level.

          So, when finally testing the game engine out for what it is, I'm pretty disappointed. YES, the monster was higher level than me - so? This isn't an MMO where anything 5 levels above you insta-kills you and you do no damage at all because of an artificial cap. And people saying "you're supposed to use bombs and oils!" can say that all day, except that only certain ones work in boss fights, and I didn't have the recipes for the ones needed here. I've been mixing side content more than evenly with story - so is it my fault I don't have them yet?

          Keep in mind: the game showed this mission as GREEN in my menu despite being 5 levels above where I'm at, so I thought it would be fine to try. I've purposely avoided everything red.

          Honestly? The only issues with the fight were that 1. the camera gets locked against walls (JUST like dark Souls/Bloodborne) and 2. the dodge/rolls have no invincibility frames (Which I would at least expect a few in a modern game IF you time it right). 

          Did I do anything wrong in the fight? No, not at all. I got frustrated, lost a few times, learned the pattern, won through determination.

          BUT it did expose the flaws of the combat engine: it's too simple, a bit wonky (lock-on doesn't work well as it does NOT actually "lock on" a-la Dark Souls) and doesn't really allow for you to challenge yourself and tackle high-level stuff. Instead, the game expects you to do tons of side content or focus on story to reach the same/near level of the hunts you want to do and just win that way rather than through determination/mastery of the combat engine, because there's not much to master. Again - probably would have steamrolled the fight if I'd had the oils/bombs recommended in the bestiary but I hadn't found them yet, probably bc I'm 5 levels below the quest.

           

          So everyone who wants to be an armchair quarterback and say "I did it wrong" can stuff it, because I haven't seen one legitimate criticism above of my gameplay. "You should have used oils/bombs" when I haven't come across them yet, as well as "you shoudl't tackle anything higher than your level" are criticisms of THE GAME ENGINE itself, not me. So stop misplacing blame. I don't actually think there was anything whatsoever wrong, I actually enjoyed finally winning over the monster after that long of a fight; you'll just turn anything into a negative for some reason.
